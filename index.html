Product Designer, Edtech

Sophia Pinto

Former Title I teacher turned designer, building tools that prioritize confidence over measurement.

In a low-performing public school in Atlanta, I watched color-coded diagnostic charts teach 8- and 10-year-olds whether they were "smart" or "dumb." They believed it, and somewhere along the way stopped feeling capable or enjoying learning. I watched my students with disabilities give up a little more each year. Administration needed test scores; I needed something that could do both: build a kid's belief that they're capable and show the growth adults are watching for.
